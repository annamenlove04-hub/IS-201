Paris, France

The City of Light, Art, and Gastronomy

Explore the Landmarks

A City Steeped in History & Elegance

Paris is more than a destination; it's a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture.

Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

Whether you're strolling through the historic Marais district, exploring world-renowned museums, or savoring a fresh croissant at a sidewalk bistro, Paris promises an unforgettable journey of discovery.

Must-Visit Landmarks

La Tour Eiffel

Completed in 1889 for the World's Fair, the Eiffel Tower was initially criticized by leading French artists, but has since become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world.

Musée du Louvre

The world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. Home to some of the most celebrated works of art, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo, housed in the grand Louvre Palace.

Arc de Triomphe

Standing at the western end of the Champs-Élysées, this monument honors those who fought and died for France in the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars, offering stunning panoramic views of the city's twelve radiating avenues.

The Art of Parisian Gastronomy

French gastronomy is so highly regarded that it was added to UNESCO's intangible cultural heritage list. In Paris, food is an art form. From the daily ritual of buying fresh baguettes to dining in world-class Michelin-starred restaurants, culinary experiences are central to the Parisian lifestyle.

Boulangeries & Pâtisseries: Fresh croissants, pains au chocolat, and macarons.
Sidewalk Café Culture: Relaxed dining and people-watching.
Fine Dining: Haute cuisine crafted by world-class chefs.
